Columbia Daily Spectator Sports Staff

12/14/22

Archery

2018: Archery wins second straight compound national title

Columbia women’s archery finished its season on May 19, 2018 with a second consecutive national title in the compound division at the National Outdoor Collegiate Archery Championships. This was the team’s third national title in four years. Sophia Strachan, CC ’20, a member of the compound team, would go on to earn a spot on the 2019 U.S. archery team. – Heather Chen

Basketball

2014-18: Camille Zimmerman shatters Columbia record books

As a Lion, Camille Zimmerman, CC ’18, was one of the most prolific players in not only the Ivy League but also the entire nation. Zimmerman’s historic career featured an otherworldly junior season in which she averaged 22.5 points and 9.6 rebounds per game. She currently holds the records in career points, field goals made, free throw percentage, games played, and games started. After graduating from Columbia, Zimmerman signed a contract with the Minnesota Lynx. She now plays professionally in the premier women's basketball league of Finland. – Matt Kim

2020: Women’s basketball qualifies for first Ivy League Tournament

After finishing the 2019-20 season 17-10 overall and 8-6 in Ancient Eight play, the Lions secured their first Ivy League Tournament bid in program history. However, due to the pandemic, their historic entry to the tournament was put on pause. The Light Blue continued its momentum after the pandemic and once again earned an Ivy League Tournament bid last season. The team found some success in the tournament, defeating Yale 67-38, but ultimately lost in the championship to Princeton. — Caleb Dye

2022: Abbey Hsu breaks Columbia career record and Ivy League single-season record

Photo by Caitlin Buckley

In only her sophomore season, junior guard Abbey Hsu, the Lions’ top three-point shooter, broke both Columbia’s three-point career record and the Ivy League three-point season record in the same game. Hsu finished the 2021-22 season with 108 three-pointers, and she will continue to add to her career total with two seasons left in a Light Blue uniform. – Rebecca Wachen

2022: Columbia women’s basketball reaches WNIT quarterfinals

Down by 17 against Boston College in the third round of the Women’s National Invitation Tournament, the 2021-22 Columbia women’s basketball team pulled off a shocking comeback to advance to WNIT quarterfinals. Despite not making a single three-pointer all night, the Lions outscored the Eagles in the second half by 18 points to keep their season alive. – Rebecca Wachen

Photo by Caitlin Buckley

2021-22: Breaking the records for wins, conference wins, points, three-pointers, rebounds, and assists

The 2021-22 season was historic for Columbia women’s basketball, which broke team records at every turn. With 25 total wins and 12 conference wins, the Lions shattered two program records. Since the addition of Hsu, Columbia’s offense has risen to another level. Hsu broke Columbia’s record of three-pointers made in a single season, contributing to the team’s record-breaking 246 triples, 63 more than the 2017-18 season. – Adam Lang

Fencing

Photo courtesy of Columbia Athletics

2018: Iman Blow becomes first Black woman in Columbia history to win NCAA individual title

When Iman Blow, CC ’19, won the final touch against Notre Dame fencer Sabrina Massialas in 2018, she broke ground as the first Black woman in Columbia history to win an NCAA individual title with her national foil victory. Blow’s title added to her ascendant resume, which already included a two-time team national champion as well as a two-time first-team All-American title. – Miles Schachner

2019: Sylvie Binder and Anne Cebula claim individual titles

In 2019, Sylvie Binder, CC ’22, and Anne Cebula, BC ’20, won the individual national titles in women’s foil and épée, respectively, to lead Columbia to its 16th national championship. Binder began her sophomore-year campaign with a 20-bout win streak and ended the season atop the podium with a 19-3 record at the NCAA tournament. Cebula’s individual title was the first women’s épée crown in program history, as well as the first-ever individual championship won by any Barnard athlete. – Robert Gao

Field Hockey

2019: Field hockey beats two top-25 teams back-to-back, first win against a ranked team

In 2019, for the first time in program history, Columbia defeated a top-25 team after a 4-3 overtime win against No. 22 Boston University. Allison Smith, CC ’22, provided the late-game heroics for the Lions, scoring a walk-off goal off a fast break with 16 seconds left in overtime. Following the win, the Light Blue took on No. 24 University of Maine the next day. After scoring three first-half goals, Columbia held off the Black Bears, defeating them 3-2 and earning its second victory in a row against a ranked opponent in just as many days. – Carlos Gonzalez-Romero

2021: Field hockey starts 6-0—its best start in program history

In Katie DeSandis’, CC ’13, first season as head coach and the Lions’ first season back from the COVID-19 pandemic, field hockey won the first six games of its season. During the six-game stretch, Columbia outscored its opponents 22-6. For the rest of the season, however, the Lions only won two more games and finished 8-9, placing seventh in the Ivy League. Despite this, their 6-0 start in 2021 was the best start in program history. – Carlos Gonzalez-Romero

2021: Kelsey Farkas named to the national team

Kelsey Farkas, CC ’22, became the first player in Columbia field hockey’s history to be named to the U.S national team. During her time at Columbia, Farkas was named captain and recorded 16 goals, 13 assists, and 45 points. As a Lion, Farkas earned multiple honors, including two-time first-team NFHCA All Mideast Region, two-time first-time All Ivy, NFHCA National Player of the week, and second-team NFHCA All Mideast Region. Farkas made her first national team appearance on Nov. 26, 2021 against Canada. – Carlos Gonzalez-Romero

Photo by Violet Mendelsund

Golf

2014: Michelle Piyapattra, CC ’14, awarded Connie S. Maniatty Award

The most accomplished golfer in Columbia history, Michelle Piyapattra was the program’s only athlete to earn three first-team All-Ivy honors and won the individual Ivy League title in 2011. In her sophomore campaign, she was named 2012 Ivy League Player of the Year. In her senior year, Columbia awarded her with the Connie S. Maniatty Award, given to a senior female athlete who exemplifies the pinnacle of athletic ability. In 2012, she was selected to compete in the NCAA Championship, becoming the first female golfer to do so in school history. Currently, she holds 16 career and season records for the Lions, including scoring average, overall wins, and finish percentage. – Matthew Rowell

Lacrosse

2022: Silver Anniversary best players

To celebrate the 25th anniversary of the women’s lacrosse program at Columbia, members of the public voted on the best players in program history. Kelsey Gedin, CC ’18, received 88 percent of the votes, the most by a wide margin. In her four years as a starter, Gedin tied the all-time program save record with 532 saves and led the Lions to their first Ivy League Tournament appearance. As a rookie, she was awarded All-Ivy Second Team accolades. She supplemented this impressive list with Division I Academic Honor Roll and Ivy League Honorable Mention awards later in her unforgettable college career. – Sydney Stoller

Rowing

2013: Samantha Warren named first-team All-American

In 2013, Samantha Warren, CC ’13, a staple of the team for four years, was named first-team All-American. She had been previously named second-team All-American. She was instrumental in the Lions’ success that season, as her efforts brought the team five victories, a regatta win, and a 15th ranking on the coaches’ poll. – Lucas Metzger

Soccer

2017: Columbia soccer wins 11-0 against Wagner University

Columbia women’s soccer crushed Wagner University 11-0 on Sept. 24, 2017. Six different Lions scored, highlighted by a hat trick from forward Emma Anderson. Columbia and Wagner have not played again since. – Julian Silva-Forbes

Softball

2018: Taylor Troutt, CC ’18, named Ivy League Player of the Year

Taylor Troutt, CC ’18, was named the Ivy League Player of the Year, the first player to receive this honor in Columbia softball’s history. Troutt, a second baseman, set the Light Blue’s single-season record for batting average, 0.455, on-base percentage, 0.507, and slugging percentage, 0.797. She has the seventh highest batting average in Ivy League history. – Annelie Hyatt

2019: Coach Teague named Ivy League Coach of the Year

Columbia softball head coach Jen Teague led the Lions in their most winningest season and first postseason run in 2019. As a result, Teague was named Ivy League Coach of the Year. The Lions had an overall record of 26-19, and four members of the team were named to the First Team All-Ivy. – Jorge Hernandez

2022: Raquel Reyes’ no-hitter versus Brown in 2022 season

In a game against Brown in her sophomore year, pitcher Raquel Reyes, CC ’24, became the fourth Lion to throw a no-hitter in Columbia softball’s 22-year history. She only allowed two base runners throughout her total 83-pitch effort and is the first no-hitter in over 10 years after Maggie Johnson, CC ’11, did it in 2011 against Princeton. Reyes was perfect against Brown through the first three innings, and the Lions were able to ride her arm to a victory against the Bears. – Alexander Kwok

Squash

2017: Colette Sultana, CC ’17, wins national title for squash

The squash team’s first and only title at the national level came from Colette Sultana, CC ’17, at the College Squash Association Individual Championships in 2017. After losing the first game of her match, she was able to recover, winning three straight games to secure victory and play for the B Draw of the Holleran West Championship. Because of her success, she secured a spot on the second All-American team. – Caleb Dye

Photo courtesy of Columbia Athletics

2020: Habiba Mohamed, CC ’22, becomes first squash player to receive All-American honors

Despite Columbia squash’s brief history as a varsity team—gaining recognition in 2010—the team has been able to recruit top prospects like Habiba Mohamed. Before coming to Columbia, Mohamed won the 2014 World Junior Squash Championship. Mohamed played an integral role in helping the program’s national reputation. In her junior year, Mohamed became the first player to receive first-team All-American honors in program history. She capped off her time with Lions by having a perfect 9-0 season. – Caleb Dye

Swimming & Diving

Photo courtesy of Columbia Athletics

2013: Kate Meili, CC ’13 shatters records

Swimmer Katie Meili, CC ’13, exploded onto the scene in the 2012-13 season, setting Columbia records for five races: the 100-meter freestyle, 200-meter intermediate medley., 400-meter intermediate medley, 100-meter breaststroke, and 200-meter breaststroke. —Julian Silva-Forbes

Tennis

2012-13: Nicole Bartnik, CC ’13, wins back-to-back Ivy League Player of the Year selections

For both the 2012 and 2013 seasons, Columbia tennis player Nicole Bartnik, CC ’13, earned Ivy League Player of the Year honors. Bartnik became just the third women’s player in Ivy League history to earn the award in two straight seasons. – Rebecca Wachen

2013: Coach Ilene Weintraub, CC ’02, winningest women’s tennis coach in Columbia history, leads team to first Ivy League championship

Women’s tennis head coach Ilene Weintraub, CC ’02, is a dedicated Lion. A two-time captain and All-Ivy League selection, Weintraub was always a threat on the tennis court at Columbia. After a successful career, Weintraub went onto become an assistant coach at Columbia, and was eventually named head coach of women’s tennis in 2007. In her 12 complete seasons, Weintraub has become the winningest head coach in Columbia women’s tennis history. In 2013, she led the Lions to their first Ivy League championship, a day she considers one of the best in her life. – Rebecca Wachen

Photo courtesy of Columbia Athletics

Track and Field

2013: Marvellous Iheukwumere, CC ’14, winning on and off the track

Marvellous Iheukwumere was honored her senior year as Ivy League Woman of the Year after winning the Ivy League 200-meter race and the 100-meter race at the Princeton Elite Meet. She is also an Arthur Ashe Sports Scholar and boasted a 3.86 GPA her junior year. – Takashi Williams

2017: Natalie Tanner, CC ’17, wins Ivy League championship as Academic All-American

Natalie Tanner won many competitions as a cross country runner and was named Ivy League champion in the outdoor 10,000 meter and a finalist in the 5,000 meter and 10,000 meter in 2017. As a result, she was named All-Ivy League. Tanner accomplished all of this whilst maintaining a high enough GPA to make her a two-time USTFCCCA Academic All-American. – Takashi Williams

Photo by Columbia Daily Spectator

Volleyball

2019: Chichi Ikwuazom, CC ’19, makes volleyball program history

In 2019, Chichi Ikwuazom posted the most decorated individual single season in Columbia volleyball history. The senior middle blocker averaged 5.32 kills and 6.02 points per set, good for third in the nation, while also hitting .438 throughout the season. Ikwuazom also averaged 1.14 blocks per set en route to 106 total for the season. For her efforts, Ikwuazom was named Ivy League Player of the Year with a unanimous selection to the All-Ivy League first team. Beyond the in-conference awards, she was also selected to the AVCA All-Northeast Region Team and earned an AVCA All-American honorable mention—both firsts in program history. – Brian Lee

Photo by KJ Mulam

Wrestling

2020: The women’s wrestling club makes Ivy League history

In early 2020, Bri Csontos, CC ’21, and Talia Fine, CC ’23, with the help of former Columbia College Alumni Association president Kyra Tirana Barry, CC ’87, formed the first women’s wrestling club in Ivy League history. While Columbia boasts a long men’s wrestling history, this achievement marks the school’s commitment to expanding opportunities to women in wrestling. With members ranging from international competitors to eager rookies, Columbia finds itself at the forefront of the surging women’s wrestling movement in its quest for varsity status. – Bernard Wang
