The 50 greatest Columbia women’s sports moments

Columbia Daily Spectator Sports Staff

December 14, 2022

Illustration by Kelsea Petersen

Letter from the editor:

Fifty years after the passage of Title IX, Spectator’s sports staff looked for a way to celebrate the decades of achievement, hard work, and sacrifice exhibited by Columbia’s women athletes. We decided to pick 50 moments over the past few decades that symbolize some of each team and individual’s most outstanding triumphs.

While Title IX was ratified in 1972, banning educational discrimination based on sex, our 50 moments mostly come from 1983 onwards—the first year Columbia enrolled women and the year Columbia and Barnard made history by establishing an athletic consortium, the first of its kind. The events range from recent history—like women’s basketball’s ascendant 2022 season—to 35 years ago, like Katy Bilodeau’s second national fencing championship in 1987.

Though we are thrilled to celebrate the Lions’ achievements, it is also important to recognize the equality gap between men’s and women’s sports. Women who participate in collegiate athletics still deal with inadequate facilities, disparities in funding, gender-based harassment, and athletic industries that pay men far more than women. For all these reasons and more, it is crucial that we continue to center women’s sports in our coverage, celebrate women’s athletic achievements, and highlight inequality whenever possible.

We hope you enjoy reading!

Miles Schachner, sports editor
